Livia Hu

Designing Accessible Digital Experiences

As a University of Toronto student, Livia Hu bridges inclusive design with creative communication to make complex ideas accessible. Her work is driven by a commitment to sustainability, mental health, and child development, and she brings together technical tools—Python, Adobe Creative Suite—and the power of storytelling to create user‑centered digital experiences grounded in research and empathy.

Featured Work

  • Human–Computer Interaction (HCI)

    Livia is deeply invested in human‑computer interaction and human‑centered design, focusing on how digital products and services can become more intuitive, equitable, and effortless for all users. Her work in HCI emphasizes accessibility, empathy, and the importance of designing with—not just for—diverse communities.

  • Sustainability

    She also approaches sustainability as a broad, future‑oriented philosophy rather than a purely environmental concern. For Livia, sustainability shapes how individuals, organizations, and communities grow, adapt, and imagine their futures. It encompasses environmental responsibility but also extends to social well‑being, ethical decision‑making, and long‑term resilience.

  • Where They Intersect

    Together, HCI and sustainability form the foundation of Livia’s forward‑looking mindset. She sees both as essential frameworks for shaping personal growth, guiding business development, and supporting societal progress. Across her projects, she blends research, design, and real‑world impact to demonstrate that sustainable thinking and inclusive design are for everyone—anyone who envisions a better future for themselves or for the communities they care about.

  • Her Projects

    Across her projects, Livia brings these commitments to life through work that blends research, design, and real‑world impact. She has explored emotional realism and ethical risks in AI relationships, prototyped inclusive transit tools for users with cerebral palsy, evaluated accessibility gaps in retail self‑checkout systems, and analyzed climate data to support equitable infrastructure planning. Each project reflects her belief that thoughtful design and sustainable thinking can meaningfully improve how people navigate their environments, access information, and imagine better futures.
